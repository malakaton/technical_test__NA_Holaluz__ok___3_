1 - Our CTO keeps saying sticky sessions are bad and they should not exist. But are they that bad? What are the effects of enabling / disabling them?

The benefits of sticky sessions are that the requests made by a specific session are routed on the same server. Is a  guarantee for pending requests don't be lost(Example e-commerce: a customer put a item in the basket and wants to continue buying, staying in a sticky session, he would not lose what he has added in the basket). This practice can become bad if, for example, in the same session we make many requests can overload a server and have bad load distribuiton between servers
